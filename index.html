Abstract

Achieving consistent and accurate visual reconstruction of human anatomy in arbitrary poses, including 3D mesh and internal skeletal information, is a valuable task with high demands for precision and authenticity in some low-cost medical scenarios. To address this task, we propose a regression-optimization framework in this work, which simultaneously predicts human body mesh and anatomical skeletal structure from a single image. The framework, named STIO (See Through the Inside and Outside), establishes a tightly association between the human body and skeleton, combining the advantages of regression and optimization methods to achieve both precision and consistency requirements, as well as good performance in terms of speed. To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to propose a visual method for anatomical skeleton and human body reconstruction in arbitrary poses using a single image. Compared to existing pose prediction or skeletal reconstruction algorithms, our method considers anatomical constraints, resulting in better accuracy, consistency, and a representation that closely resembles real human bodies, meeting the requirements of medical-related scenarios.
